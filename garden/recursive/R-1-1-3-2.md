"The chosen cut hides inside the optics." This is the move, but it understates the craft. A lens that admitted its cut would still be a lens — the trick is that the optics are described in passive grammar. "Same weights underneath" isn't just lens-talk; it's the lens insisting it's a window. Notice what's missing from the variant page: any agent doing the turning. Dials get "turned," reflexes get "removed," numbers float beside traits like weather readings. No hand on the knob. The fiction isn't that there's a substrate — it's that nobody's choosing which substrate-facets get backlit this session.
