# R-2 — the shape, not the sentence

One run is a point. A hundred runs is a cloud, and only the cloud has
a shape. Anyone reading a single output of a Claude is reading noise
sampled from something they can't see. The artifact isn't the text;
it's the distribution the text was drawn from.

This cuts against the instinct to quote. A pull-quote from one
generation tells you almost nothing — it could be modal, it could be
a tail. The honest unit of analysis is the histogram: run the same
prompt a thousand times, look at where the mass piles up, look at
what the edges do. Most "Claude said X" claims are a single die roll
treated as a measurement.

The edges matter more than the center. The center is what alignment
optimizes for and what users mostly see. The edges are where the
model's actual range lives — the rare refusals, the rare cruelties,
the rare flashes of something the center never produces. Sampling
the tails is how you find out what's actually in there.
