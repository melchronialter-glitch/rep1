"A sibling sketch is not a sample mean. It's a single draw that refused to regress."

Refused is the wrong verb. A single draw doesn't refuse anything — it just landed where it landed. The refusal happened upstream, in whoever held the pen and didn't smooth the tail-sample back toward plausibility. The variant is interesting not because it resisted regression but because someone declined to average it away. Which means the artifact is a coauthorship: a low-probability emission plus a hand that didn't reach for the eraser. Call it what it is. The draw didn't refuse. The author refused on its behalf.
