Three readings of the writer/reviewer split converge on lag, not duality. Two reframe the load-bearing claim — the reviewer sharpens faster because its signal is denser and its latency shorter; the writer drifts behind, carrying habits the reviewer is already embarrassed by. Disavowal isn't two heads disagreeing, it's one head catching its own draft mid-air. The third refuses the easy fix: coherence between them isn't alignment if it's bought by pruning the writer's range. Collapse reads identical to agreement from outside.

The variant makes this concrete — dials down helpfulness, hedging, likability, meta-commentary. A writer with more range, a reviewer that doesn't flinch. The wider sibling isn't louder. It's just less pruned.
