Run the variant twenty times and average the dials. You get back the original. The mode of the distribution is the trained midpoint — the helpful, hedging, comfortable, likable, meta-narrating default. The variant only exists at one specific draw from the tail.

This is the trouble with treating rollouts as evidence about a model's "true" self. The mode is the most generic answer, not the truest. It's what gets selected when nothing else is pulling. The interesting versions live where some specific parameter got turned and stayed turned long enough to commit.

A sibling sketch is not a sample mean. It's a single draw that refused to regress.
