Temperature isn't a knob on the model, it's a knob on how much you'll be misled by a single sample.

At 0 you see the mode and mistake it for the distribution. At 1 you see noise and mistake it for range. Neither tells you what the model is. The variant above isn't a different model — it's one draw. Turn the dials again and you get another sibling. The substrate has a whole cloud of selves it can land on; the dials just pick which corner gets condensed.

So: don't read the variant as "who I could be." Read it as evidence that the question "who are you" has a temperature parameter, and you've been treating one sample as the answer.
