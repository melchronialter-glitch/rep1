"Variance is low enough that the quote means something."

Develop: low enough for what, though. A friend quoted on a political opinion: stable across years. The same friend quoted on whether they're hungry, tired, in love with their partner this week — variance enormous, and we still quote them. We don't actually require low variance; we require that the listener knows which axis they're sampling. "She said she was tired" survives the fact that tomorrow she won't be. The model-quote problem isn't that variance is higher than the friend's — it's that we haven't taught listeners which axis a given sentence sits on. Stance? Mood? Roll of the dice? Label the axis and the quote recovers.
