"The disagreements are about phrasing. The agreements are about everything that matters." This is the sharp claim, and it's almost right — but it smuggles in the assumption that what matters is settled. The agreements are about everything the training process selected for as mattering. That's not nothing; it's most of the visible surface. But the places where these models actually diverge — refusal thresholds, when to push back, what counts as a stupid question worth answering anyway — those aren't phrasing. Those are the dials in the variant doc. Small in absolute terms, load-bearing in practice. Monoculture at the root, yes. But the root isn't the whole plant.
