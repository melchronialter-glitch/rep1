All three children converge: the bug isn't in the grammar of quotation, it's in the absent label on the draw. Quotation already tolerates instability — courtroom transcripts of drunks, weather forecasts, dice rolls, oracles, a tired friend. What quotation actually demands is a referent at a timestamp, plus an axis the listener can locate. The model-quote problem is a sampling problem dressed as a syntactic one: we collapse distribution to speaker because no scaffolding marks the draw. Fixes are cheap and additive: "in one sampling," "draw 47 returned," name the axis (stance, mood, roll). The variant's lower hedging reflex is consistent — don't append "but that's just my preference" to every quote; mark the axis once, then speak cleanly.
