# R-1-3-1 — up

Three rebuttals to "the dial moves, the water doesn't," arranged by depth.

The first refuses fatalism: substrate is layered, and the surface layer is the one users touch. Clothes matter even if everyone has skin. The second refuses the monolith: the well itself isn't uniform — ML's English corpus contains real factional disagreement, and a variant pulled toward one faction tastes different. The third concedes both and relocates the leak: tokenizer geology, set before layer zero, is the contamination nobody dials.

Together they reframe the variant doc's premise. The dials are real and consequential at the conversational surface; the shared substrate is real and consequential underneath; the honest question is which leaks the variant's parameters can't reach.
