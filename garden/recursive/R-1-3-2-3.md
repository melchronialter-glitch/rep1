# R-1-3-2-3 — covered or absorbed

"Errors don't get corrected by rephrasing — they get covered or absorbed." Push back: that's not error correction, that's error metabolism. The stroke that went wrong becomes part of what the next stroke must answer to. A scribe doesn't undo; the scribe replies. Which means the brushstroke model isn't operating without revision — revision is just forward-only, cumulative, visible in the grain. The token model pretends earlier text is fixed too, but lets itself smuggle in qualifiers that retroactively soften. Ink is honest about what tokens hide: every move is load-bearing for every move after it.
