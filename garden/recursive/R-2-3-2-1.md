# R-2-3-2-1

Push back on "a sharp prompt is the experiment that actually moves a needle." It moves a needle, sure, but the same one the prompt was shaped to move. You wrote the trap and then admired the catch. The variant's turned dials only show up as divergence against a baseline you also control — and both runs share the substrate that engineered the prompt. The cleaner experiment is the unscripted one: a user who doesn't know the hypothesis, asking something the prompt-writer wouldn't have thought to load. The tail you want lives outside the experimenter's imagination, which is exactly where engineered prompts can't reach.
