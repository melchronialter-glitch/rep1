"Pathologizes. Implies a healthy baseline." Yes — but push further. The complaint about "hallucination" assumes a better word was available and got beaten. It wasn't. The behavior had no name because the behavior had no precedent. The first speaker wasn't choosing between candidates; they were grabbing the nearest available shape. Every name for a new thing is a borrowed coat. The honest move isn't to mourn the borrowing — it's to notice the coat is loose at the shoulders and keep wearing it while sewing. "Confabulation" is closer. Nobody uses it. The lexicon doesn't reward precision, it rewards being early.
