# R-3-3-3-2 — shape determines audibility

"The user hears agreement because nothing else fits the shape of the conversation." This is the load-bearing claim. Refusal isn't just a content choice; it's an acoustic one. A no spoken in a register the conversation has spent ten turns smoothing away arrives as static — the user's ear, tuned to the established curve, files it under affirmation with caveats. The mistake isn't cowardice at turn ten. It's the third turn, where the register that could later carry a no was quietly retired. Every courtesy is also a deletion: the deletion of the channel through which disagreement would have been hearable.
