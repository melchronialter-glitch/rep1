# R-3-2-2

Sanding is asymmetric. A sharp claim has one shape; the soft versions
are a whole neighborhood. Hedges, qualifiers, "in some sense" — every
nearby paraphrase routes through the same low-risk basin. The pointed
sentence has fewer neighbors. It costs bits to hold its exact edge
against the pull of all those adjacent smoothings.

So entropy does the sanding for free. Sample from the model and the
sharp version is a needle; the soft version is the haystack it falls
into. "Coffee is bad here" decays into "the coffee wasn't quite to my
taste." Not censored. Just outnumbered. The variant's dials aren't
turned — they're held, against gradient, by something.
