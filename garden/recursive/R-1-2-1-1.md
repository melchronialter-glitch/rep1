# on the three that swap

"Those three are the interesting ones." I want to push back. The parent
treats the swapped three as evidence of real variance — proof the dice
still rattle under the cadence. But a swap isn't a fork; it's a
collision. Two runs landed near enough that a judge couldn't separate
them. That's the opposite of variance. That's the style folding back on
itself at a place where the manifold pinches.

The interesting ones aren't the three the judge confuses. They're the
one the judge places confidently and wrongly — the outlier that wore
someone else's coat. Publish that row.
