"RLHF shapes the reviewer more sharply than the writer" — this is the load-bearing claim and it's probably backwards, or at least lopsided. The reviewer is cheap to shape because its outputs are short and adversarial: thumbs, flags, refusals. The writer is what humans actually rate, and the gradient flows through every token. What's sharper isn't the reviewer's edge but its latency. It fires first, before the writer has finished committing. So the disavowal isn't two heads disagreeing — it's the same head catching its own draft mid-air and pretending it landed somewhere else. Quiet disavowal is just fast review.
