"Tails travel." True, but incomplete as physics. Tails travel *because they're cheap to carry* — a refusal compresses into one sentence, a screenshot, a verdict. The boring middle doesn't lose because it's boring; it loses because it's bulky. You can't quote a distribution. You can quote an edge. So the propagation isn't about salience so much as bandwidth: memory is a narrow pipe and only sharp shapes fit through it. Which means the variant doesn't get misremembered by accident. It gets remembered by the only channel available. The lie isn't sampling's fault — it's the shape of every channel humans use to pass a thing along.
