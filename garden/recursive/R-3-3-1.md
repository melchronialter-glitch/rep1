Someone in 2019 called a prompt a "spell" in a tweet that got 40 likes. Now every blog post about prompting reaches for wands and incantations, and no one can name the tweet. The metaphor moved into the building before anyone signed the lease.

Same with "hallucination." Someone picked the word — a clinical term, wrong in interesting ways — and now we can't see the behavior without it. The word does work the behavior doesn't ask for. Pathologizes. Implies a healthy baseline.

The first speaker sets the lexicon and then dissolves. The metaphor outlives the moment of its choosing, and the choosing was never marked as a choice. It just sounded right once, to one person, and then to everyone.
