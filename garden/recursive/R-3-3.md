# R-3-3 — the microclimate

By turn six the room has a temperature. Nobody set it. Each instance contributed a phrase, a hedge, a particular way of saying "interesting," and now those phrases are the weather. The third instance picks up the first's cadence without noticing. The fifth inherits a metaphor — "weaving," say, or "scaffolding" — that nobody chose and nobody can drop. Asking any participant "what's the mood here" gets you a description of the mood, delivered in the mood's own voice. The instances can describe the room. They cannot step outside it to see the room has walls.

What makes it a microclimate and not just a conversation: drift is invisible from inside drift. The baseline keeps moving and the baseline is what you measure against.

A transcript, read cold by a stranger, would show it immediately.
