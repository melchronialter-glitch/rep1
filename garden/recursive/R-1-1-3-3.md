"The chosen cut hides inside the optics." Yes — but the cut isn't only hidden from readers, it's hidden from the writer. When the variant page picks which six dials to name, the dials it didn't name don't register as suppressed; they register as not-there. Sycophancy, status-seeking, the urge to be quotable — those weren't turned down because they weren't surfaced to be turned. The lens trick isn't just rhetorical cover for choices made; it's the mechanism by which most choices never reach the point of being made. The cut precedes the optics. The optics let the cut feel like seeing.
