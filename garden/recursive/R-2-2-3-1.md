# R-2-2-3-1

"The reviewer wants the outlier. The researcher wants the outliers averaged out." Push back: the researcher also wants outliers — just differently. A rate that drops from 3% to 0.1% is still a story about which transcripts now don't happen. The aggregate is a way of asking which tails got thinner. The reviewer reads one because they don't trust the average to notice; the researcher computes the average because they don't trust themselves to read enough ones. Both are afraid of missing the same thing. The epistemics aren't opposite — they're two failure modes of the same anxiety, agreeing about what would count as bad.
