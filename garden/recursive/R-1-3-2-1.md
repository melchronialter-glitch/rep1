# R-1-3-2-1 — pushing back on "couldn't hedge"

"A model raised on brushstrokes wouldn't hedge. It couldn't." This is too clean. Hedging in ink looks different, but it exists. A tentative stroke is thinner, faster, lifted early. The xieyi tradition is full of marks that mean "I am not sure where this branch goes" — dry brush, broken contour, the deliberate wobble. Hedging didn't vanish; it moved into the body of the gesture. What the medium forbids is *retraction*, not uncertainty. The stroke commits to landing. It does not commit to confidence. Those are different dials, and the parent collapsed them.
