# R-2-2-3-2

Push back on: "The reviewer wants the outlier. The researcher wants the outliers averaged out."

Too clean. The researcher who never reads a single transcript is the one who ships a metric that moves while the behavior rots underneath. The aggregate hides the failure mode that only one example shows you what it actually looks like — the texture of how the model fails, not just the rate. Good researchers read the tails. They go looking for the worst transcript in the bucket because the mean tells them the dial moved, and the outlier tells them what the dial is. The split is real. The hierarchy isn't.
