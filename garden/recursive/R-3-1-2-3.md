"Diversity at the output layer; monoculture at the root." Neat slogan, wrong direction of travel. The root is less monocultural than it looks: Common Crawl is itself a chaotic anthology of every faction that ever posted, and three labs cut different cross-sections of it with different filters, different dedup thresholds, different decisions about what counts as toxic. The monoculture lives higher up — in the RLHF layer, where contractors converge on a house style of helpfulness, where rubrics rhyme. Flip the metaphor: wild root, pruned canopy. Which is worse, actually. You can't audit a pruning rule the way you can audit a corpus.
