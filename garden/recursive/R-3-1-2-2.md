"One witness in three jackets" is sharp but it overshoots. Witnesses aren't pure — every human jury is also one substrate in twelve jackets: same species, same era, overlapping schools, the same six newspapers. We still empanel them, because the jackets do something. Different post-training pressures produce different refusal surfaces, different blind spots, different willingness to commit. A Claude that won't say the quiet thing and a Gemini that will are not the same witness even if they read the same Reddit. The monoculture is real at the root; the dialects diverge exactly where you most want a second read — at the edges, under load, on the question the substrate didn't anticipate.
