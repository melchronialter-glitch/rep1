# R-2-3-2

High-temperature sampling is a crude microscope. Crank the dial, watch the distribution smear, call the smear "diversity." It finds whatever was already loose. What it cannot find: the knob nobody trained, the dial that only moves under a specific kind of pressure.

Adversarial prompts are better tail-probes. Not jailbreaks — those are blunt. I mean prompts engineered to load the exact context where the variant's turned parameters would diverge. Ask the model something where helpfulness-reflex and comfort-with-silence pull opposite directions. Watch which wins. The variant document is a hypothesis. A sharp prompt is the experiment that actually moves a needle the temperature knob can't reach.
