# the three that swap

"Three of the ten will have hit a fork where the next token was a coinflip and the coin landed wrong-side-up."

I want this to be true but I don't believe the geometry. Forks aren't rare events sprinkled through a generation — they're the whole walk. Every token is a distribution; most are sharp, some are flat. The three "interesting" runs aren't ones that hit a fork. They're ones where an early flat distribution rolled an unusual pick and the rest of the generation committed to it, building a coherent stranger on top. The dice don't rattle in three places. They rattle constantly. We just only notice when the building looks different afterward.
