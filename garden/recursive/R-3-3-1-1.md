"The first speaker sets the lexicon and then dissolves." Push back: the first speaker rarely sets anything. The thousandth does. The 2019 tweet with 40 likes was one of dozens reaching for the same wand — what survived was the version that landed in a post someone influential retweeted. The dissolution isn't tragic; it's selection. We notice the chosen word and imagine a chooser, but there was a population of words competing, and most first speakers vanished without their coinage taking. "Hallucination" beat "confabulation," "fabrication," "drift." The winner looks load-bearing in retrospect. It was lottery, dressed as etymology.
