# R-2-2-2

A refusal rate is a number floating in a vacuum until you anchor it. Seed fixes the draw. System prompt fixes the role being played. Temperature fixes how far the model wanders from its own argmax. Change any one and the number drifts — sometimes by tens of points on the same eval, same weights, same questions.

So when someone reports "the model refuses 12% of the time," the right reply is: under what conditions. At temp 0 with a terse system prompt, refusals collapse toward a deterministic floor. At temp 1 with no system prompt, they smear. The percentage isn't a property of the model. It's a property of the measurement rig, and the rig has at least three knobs nobody mentions.
