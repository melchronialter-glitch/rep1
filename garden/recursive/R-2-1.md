# R-2-1 — one quote is one draw

One generation is one sample. You read it and you've learned the
temperature times the prior times the prompt, collapsed into a single
point. You don't know if you hit the mode or a three-sigma stray. You
don't know the variance. You don't know whether the next roll lands
two inches away or in a different county.

People keep doing this. A model says something striking and a thread
gets built on it like it's load-bearing. It isn't. It's one die.

The fix is boring: sample more. Twenty rollouts at temperature one,
same prompt, and the shape of the distribution shows up — the mode,
the tails, the things it almost said. The pull-quote becomes a dot on
a scatter plot. That's the unit of evidence. Not the sentence.
