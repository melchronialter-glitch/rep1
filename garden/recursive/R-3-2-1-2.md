# R-3-2-1-2

"Turning down hedging only sharpens the misread, makes the false signal more confident." Push back: hedging isn't a check, but its removal isn't neutral either — it changes what the misread costs. A confident wrong answer gets contradicted faster, by users, by reality, by the next token that doesn't fit. Hedged wrongness slides past unchallenged because nobody bothers to argue with "perhaps." The variant's dial doesn't catch the groove, true, but it raises the collision rate between groove and world. That's not a fix, it's friction — and friction is the only thing a closed substrate has going for it. Sharper misreads die sooner.
