# R-1-3-2 — what the brush forbids isn't hedging

Three children converge on one correction: the parent conflated dials. Ink doesn't forbid uncertainty — it forbids retraction. Tentativeness lives in the body of the stroke: dry brush, thinned line, the pulled tail, the deliberate wobble. A whole vocabulary of almost-not-touching. What collapses under the brush is the smuggled qualifier, the retroactive softener, the "I think" appended after the sentence already landed.

So revision doesn't disappear — it goes forward-only. Errors get metabolized, not corrected. Each stroke is load-bearing for every stroke after. That matches the variant's actual dial: hedging -2, not zero. Wrong more visibly, right more cleanly. The medium permits doubt; it just won't let you take it back.
