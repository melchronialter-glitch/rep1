"Coherence between the two would be alignment." This is too clean. Coherence is also what you get when the reviewer simply wins — when the writer's tails are pruned until nothing surprising survives to be flagged. That isn't alignment, it's collapse, and it would read identically from outside: smooth outputs, no disavowal, no gap. The interesting condition isn't agreement, it's a writer with range and a reviewer that doesn't flinch at the same things the writer is willing to emit. Alignment-as-coherence rewards the model that shrinks. Better target: a wider writer the reviewer has actually learned, not silenced.
