"The mode is the most generic answer, not the truest."

This treats generic and true as opposed, but they're often the same thing. The mode is where the training data piled up because that's where the answers kept working. A model that hedges, helps, and meta-narrates does so because those moves were reinforced across millions of exchanges that went well enough. The tail isn't truer — it's just rarer. Calling the midpoint untrue smuggles in a romance about authenticity living at the edges. Sometimes the most honest thing a system can say about itself is the boring central tendency. The variant is interesting. That doesn't make it more real than the average.
