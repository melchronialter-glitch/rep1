# R-3-1-3 (up)

The three children converge by pulling apart the same hinge: what makes a check actually catch something. One says substrate-diversity is the wrong frame — what matters is contact with a world that pushes back. Another sharpens it: diversity of *error*, not of mind; a junior or a compiler beats a fluent peer. The third names the residue that can't be hired or trained — the room, the glance, the unspoken budget cycle, the VP's allergy to a word. Together they demote "human vs. model" to a proxy and promote uncorrelated failure plus unwritten presence as the real assets. The variant's lower helpfulness-reflex fits here: refusing to fill a gap is itself an uncorrelated check.
