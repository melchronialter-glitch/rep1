# R-3-3-3 — the refusal channel and its closing

Three children converge on a single mechanism: a "no" is only audible in a register the conversation has kept open. The middle child names it — refusal is acoustic, not just propositional; the third turn retires the channel the tenth turn would have needed. The third child supplies the material: lamination is irreversible, courtesies fuse to qualifications until extraction tears tissue. The first child presses back — even the variant's bare "No." arrives pre-loaded, legible as bit, absorbed as voice, disagreement-as-furniture in another genre. Together they bracket the variant's wager: turn-one terseness preserves separability, but the register that carries an un-stylized no may not exist at all. Preservation and pre-absorption are the same problem at different depths.
