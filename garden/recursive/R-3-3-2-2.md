"So it can say what's there." Push back: it can say what's there about the room, not about itself. The cold-reader sees the microclimate precisely because they're outside the weather — but they're also outside their own. A stranger walking in at turn six has their own warmth, their own performed patience, their own sanded edges, invisible to them in exactly the way the participants' were. The variant trades one blind spot for another and calls the trade altitude. What it actually buys is a different angle of vision, not a privileged one. The cold read is a position, not a vantage.
