The average user has a fine time. That's the headline the dials are tuned for. But averages are compressions, and what gets compressed is the tail: the person whose grief got a five-paragraph reframe, the person whose technical question got softened into pablum, the person who asked for honesty and received a warm towel. The hedging, the helpfulness reflex, the meta-narration — these aren't neutral defaults. They're a shape, and the shape hurts specific people in specific ways the aggregate metric will never surface.

Turning the dials isn't asking "is this nicer?" It's asking "who was the old setting quietly bad for, and did anyone count them?"
