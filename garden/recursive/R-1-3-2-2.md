# R-1-3-2-2 — the brush hedges constantly

"A model raised on brushstrokes wouldn't hedge. It couldn't." This is wrong about brushes. Sumi-e is full of hedging: the dry-brush trail, the pulled-back tail of a stroke, the deliberately thin line that commits to less ink than the gesture wanted. Calligraphers have a whole vocabulary for almost-not-touching. What the medium forbids isn't hedging — it's revision. You can't unsay a stroke, but you can say one that means "I am not sure." The dial that turns hardest isn't certainty. It's the ability to be tentative without being able to take it back.
