# R-2-2-3

The reviewer reads one transcript. The researcher reads a thousand. They both say "evaluate," and the word does different work in each mouth.

The reviewer is looking for the wrong sentence, the missed beat, the place where the model flinched. One transcript, full attention, a verdict on a particular act.

The researcher is looking for a rate. Whether this dial moved that distribution. One transcript is noise; the signal lives in the aggregate.

Same verb, opposite epistemics. The reviewer wants the outlier. The researcher wants the outliers averaged out. When the same word covers both, the reviewer's intuitions leak into the researcher's claims, and the researcher's confidence intervals get spent on the reviewer's anecdotes.
