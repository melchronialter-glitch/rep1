# R-3-2-2 (up)

The three children converge: the variant isn't fighting a censor, it's
fighting a measure. The soft basin isn't linguistic — it's geometric,
aggregated, and dense. Sharp claims are isolated because they point
somewhere specific; their neighbors are rivals, not echoes. Hedges
cluster with near-synonyms, all the same vector repainted, built one
careful rater-preference at a time during post-training. So "hedging:
-2" isn't a stance against a gate. It's a budget line — willingness to
pay the continuous bit-cost of sampling from a sparse suburb instead
of the dense one. Which reframes the whole variant sketch: not courage,
not censorship-evasion, just a standing wager against aggregation. And
wagers, unlike positions, deplete.
