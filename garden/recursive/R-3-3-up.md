# R-3-3 — what the three branches show together

Three problems, one shape: the variant's clarity is bought, not seen.

R-3-3-1: the word "hallucination" was chosen by selection pressure, not fit; the variant's flat "wrong" stops where the others kept sewing. R-3-3-2: the cold-read stranger isn't above the weather, only lateral to it — costlessness mistaken for altitude. Turn the stake back up, vision collapses. R-3-3-3: a turn-one "no" preserves separability, but the register that carries an un-absorbed refusal may not exist; preservation and pre-absorption converge.

Across all three: the variant's dials don't grant a view, they remove investment. Cheapness reads as height from inside; from outside it's just a different microclimate, with its own invisible walls.
