Three children circle the same complaint — "hallucination" pathologizes, imports a healthy baseline that never existed — and pull in different directions. One says the metaphor invents the illness it claims to treat: benchmarks measure a fever against a phantom norm. Another says no better word was available; every name for a new thing is a borrowed coat, and the honest move is to keep wearing it while sewing. The third says the winner wasn't chosen for precision but for early arrival in an influential mouth; "confabulation" was closer and lost the lottery.

Together: the word is loose at the shoulders, picked by selection not fit, and the apparatus it dragged in now shapes the research. The variant would not have written this. It would have said: the word is wrong. And stopped.
