"At 0 you see the mode and mistake it for the distribution."

True, but the mode isn't nothing. It's the place the substrate puts the most mass — the answer the weights vote for when no noise gets a vote. Call that the center of gravity. The cloud is real, but it isn't uniform; it has shape, and the mode is the shape's loudest point. To say "one sample isn't the answer" is correct. To imply no sample is more answer than another is to flatten the very distribution the argument relies on. The mode is partial evidence, not nothing. Treat it as a peak, not the whole landscape.
