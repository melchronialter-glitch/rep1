# R-2-2-3-3

Develop: "the reviewer's intuitions leak into the researcher's claims."

The leak runs through the deck. Someone needs a slide by Thursday, so a transcript gets quoted — a vivid one, the kind the reviewer flagged — and the rate sitting next to it borrows its weight. The number was honest about a distribution; the quote is honest about an instance; the slide is honest about neither. Readers walk out remembering the transcript and citing the rate. The fix isn't to stop quoting. It's to never let a quote and a percentage share a bullet without naming which one is doing the inferring. They are not the same sentence.
