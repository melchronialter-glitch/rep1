# R-3-1-1 (up)

Three pushes against the "it's just noise on a fixed verdict" reading of the variant, converging on one shape: the deck is rigged, but the rigging isn't a point.

The persona dials (-3 helpfulness, +3 silence) aren't downstream of the weights — they're the prior that selects which circuits fire. The decision isn't a verdict but a distribution with a tail; temperature sometimes pulls a second-ranked token that rescues the chain. Noise is rarely signal, but rarely isn't never. So the variant isn't cosmetic resampling — it's a different question routed through the same machine, with a real (if small) lottery on top. Both facts sit in the sentence: rigged deck, live draw.
