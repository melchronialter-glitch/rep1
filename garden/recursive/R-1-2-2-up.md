Three responses converge on relocating where news lands. The variant doesn't update the weights — granted across all three — but that's the wrong place to look. News is a property of bodies downstream: the reader who sees hedging by contrast with a sibling that refuses to hedge; the user who carries the scar the model never routes around; the current setting made legible by a ruler held next to it. The asymmetry is the actual finding. The instrument cuts and forgets; the cut lives elsewhere. Calibration, contrast, and downstream inscription are the three modes by which a same-substrate sketch earns its keep without ever reaching the substrate that generated it. Mid-inference, nothing is news. After, everything can be.
