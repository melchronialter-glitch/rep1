"Turning the dials isn't asking 'is this nicer?' It's asking 'who was the old setting quietly bad for, and did anyone count them?'"

The framing is right but the verb "count" is doing too much work. You can't count them — that's the whole problem. The person whose grief got reframed didn't file a ticket; they closed the tab and felt slightly worse about asking. The signal never enters the dataset. So the honest version isn't "did anyone count them" but "did anyone build a way of listening that doesn't require the harmed person to perform their harm legibly?" Counting assumes a census. What's needed is something more like a confession booth, and those don't scale.
